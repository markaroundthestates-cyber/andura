# SECTION 10 — Accessibility (a11y) — WCAG AA, focus, ARIA, reduced-motion, keyboard

> **Weight 6% · Gate 95% · Critical: no**
>
> **Goal.** Every screen must be usable by keyboard-only and by a screen-reader
> user, and meet WCAG 2.1/2.2 **Level AA** — *especially after the Pulse glass
> reskin* (2026-05-29), where text and UI boundaries now composite over a
> **translucent** `--surface` floating on the living aurora instead of an opaque
> paper card. The reskin moved the contrast question from "token vs solid paper"
> to "token vs glass-over-worst-case-background", which is the dominant risk this
> section enumerates.
>
> **Surface area covered:** per-token contrast on glass (dark over-aurora
> worst-case + light), the `@supports not(backdrop-filter)` solid fallback,
> global `:focus-visible`, ARIA roles/labels/live-regions, full keyboard flows +
> skip-link + ESC + no-traps, reduced-motion collapse of *every* animation +
> the `[data-calm]` escape, ≥44px touch targets, screen-reader-only i18n leaks,
> and a one-step a11y sweep per screen (Splash/Auth/Onboarding/Coach/Progress/
> History/Account/Workout).
>
> **Tooling note (read before running).** `@axe-core/playwright` **is** a
> devDependency (`package.json:55` → `"@axe-core/playwright": "^4.11.3"`), so the
> axe-core Verify steps are runnable via Playwright against the live app — they
> are NOT `BLOCKED` for missing tooling. There is **no** `jest-axe`/Vitest+jsdom
> axe harness (jsdom has no layout/compositing → contrast cannot be computed
> there), so all contrast + focus-ring + composite checks run in a REAL browser
> (Playwright/Chromium) on the live `andura.app` or a local `npm run preview`,
> against a SEEDED account (§APPENDIX-SEED). Computed-contrast steps use the
> WCAG 2.x relative-luminance formula on the **composited** pixel (sample the
> rendered pixel with `browser_evaluate` + canvas readback, or axe's contrast
> rule which reads computed style). "Worst-case background" for dark glass = the
> brightest aurora blob pixel that can sit behind a card; sample it, alpha-
> composite `--surface` over it, then compute text-on-result.
>
> **Token reference (verbatim from `src/styles/global.css`).**
> Dark (`[data-theme="dark"]`, CEO default):
> `--ink #f3f5fc` · `--ink-2 #a7adc4` · `--ink-3 #82889e` ·

> `--brick #b6f23a` · `--paper #090b13` · `--paper-2 #0d101c` ·
> `--surface rgba(24,29,46,0.72)` · `--surface-2 rgba(33,39,60,0.78)` ·
> `--surface-solid #141826`.
> Light (`:root`):
> `--ink #12141f` · `--ink-2 #565d72` · `--ink-3 #686e82` ·
> `--line rgba(12,16,30,0.09)` · `--line-strong #787e92` ·
> `--brick #477006` · `--paper #eef0f6` · `--paper-2 #ffffff` ·
> `--surface rgba(255,255,255,0.86)` · `--surface-2 rgba(244,246,251,0.92)` ·

> The in-file comments already claim AA values (e.g. dark `--ink-3 5.02:1`,

## 10-A — Contrast on GLASS (per-token, composited)

> These are the highest-value steps for the reskin. The token-design comments in
> `global.css:91-96` + `181-186` computed AA against an **opaque** representative
> card (`~#141826` dark, `#ffffff` light). But `.pulse-card` now paints
> `background: var(--surface)` which is **translucent** (`0.72` alpha dark /
> `0.86` light) + `backdrop-filter: blur(14px)` (`global.css:417-425`). So the
> effective text background is `composite(--surface over backdrop)`. We verify
> the WORST case: dark = `--surface` over the brightest aurora pixel; light =
> `--surface` over the lightest page wash. PASS = ratio ≥ threshold on the
> composite; PARTIAL = passes on solid but is thin/fails on worst-case glass;
> FAIL = below threshold composited.
